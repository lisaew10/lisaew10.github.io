Hike the Gesyer

"On November 30, 1937, a well drilling operation while attempting to build a natural hot springs swimming pool was surprised when it unintentionally released Soda Springs’s famous captive geyser, which surprised everyone by shooting 100 feet into the air. It has been capped and a timer activates it once every hour on the hour. The water is about 70 degrees Fahrenheit. There is now a park and a visitor center at the site. In addition to its captive geyser, Soda Springs has an industrial lava flow from the dumping of molten rock left over from Monsanto's phosphate mining and manufacturing process one mile north of the town." Wikipedia

Downtown Hike

Caribou County's seat is Soda Springs, Idaho. Take the time to walk and appreciate this interesting small downtown. With a population of 3,058 at the 2010 census, you will not have to worry about crowds. Come enjoy the historic buildings. Make sure you stop by the oldest drug store in Idaho, Eastman Drug. It is located on 112 S Main and is complete with an old-fashioned soda fountain.
